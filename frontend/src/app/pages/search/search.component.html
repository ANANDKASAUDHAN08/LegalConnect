Your 5 Constitutional Rights During Police Arrest

1.
Right to know grounds of arrest: Police must state why you are arrested and if it is bailable (CrPC Sec 50).
2.
Right to consult a lawyer: You have a constitutional right to contact a legal counsel of your choice.
3.
Right to remain silent: You cannot be forced to confess or make self-incriminating remarks.
4.
24-hour Magistrate limit: You must be produced before a Magistrate within 24 hours of arrest (CrPC Sec 57).
5.
Women rights: Women can only be arrested by female officers and only between sunrise and sunset (CrPC Sec 46).
